How Flylisted tripled listing throughput without scaling overhead.

A Boston-based real estate marketing company operating in New England and South Florida needed faster video turnaround as listing volume grew. They plugged in Staffify video editors and admin support instead of hiring full-time in-house.

The ChallengeGrowth was outpacing the edit room.

Flylisted built its reputation on fast, polished listing media — drone, walkthrough video, and photography that gives agents a marketing edge in two of the most competitive real estate markets in the country.

The problem wasn't lead flow. It was the bottleneck behind every shoot. As volume grew through the spring listing season, the team was shipping more raw footage than their two in-house editors could turn around. Agents were waiting 2-4 days for finished video. Some were sending follow-up nudges. A few referred next month's listing to a competitor.

The instinct was to hire a third editor locally. The math didn't work. A full-time editor in Boston runs $70-90K loaded, before benefits, taxes, and the ramp time before they're producing at speed. To absorb the seasonal swing, Flylisted would have been paying a winter salary for summer demand.

The ApproachPlug in a flex editing team instead of adding W-2 headcount.

Staffify placed three vetted video editors with Flylisted, all experienced in real estate listing media. The arrangement was structured around output, not hours: editors picked up jobs from a shared queue, with QA and dispatching handled by a Staffify operations layer rather than the Flylisted leadership team.

Two design choices made it work:

  • Editors specialized inside the queue. One editor owned drone and walkthrough composites. One owned vertical social cuts for Reels and TikTok. One floated to absorb peaks. That specialization cut average edit time roughly in half.
  • The operations layer absorbed the chaos. Staffify handled QA, replacement coverage when an editor was out, and ticketing. Flylisted's founders never had to onboard, train, or troubleshoot the editing team directly.

An admin VA was added a few weeks later to handle agent intake, file collection, and the back-and-forth of scheduling reshoots. That move alone took another six hours per week off the founders' plate.

The thing that mattered most wasn't even the cost. It was that I stopped being the bottleneck. Listings ship now without me touching them.
Flylisted founders · Boston, MA

The Outcome3x volume, same leadership team, faster client experience.

Within 90 days, Flylisted was turning listings around in 12 to 24 hours instead of 2 to 4 days. Total monthly listing throughput tripled. Agents who had been on the fence about exclusivity locked into multi-listing agreements once they trusted the turnaround.

The cost line came in at roughly 32% of what the equivalent local hire would have cost — and because the Staffify arrangement scales by output rather than salary, Flylisted can ramp coverage up or down with the listing season instead of carrying winter overhead.

Most importantly: zero new salaried headcount was added. The founders kept the team lean, kept margin intact, and stopped editing in the evenings to catch up.
